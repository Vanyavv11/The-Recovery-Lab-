Week 2 · ACL Recovery Journal

The Adjustment Phase

The second week was still difficult, but the pain began changing. Swelling, quad soreness, fatigue, and pressure became the main challenges as I slowly adjusted to moving through everyday life again.

Main Challenge Quad swelling and soreness
Mobility Two crutches and brace

Week 2 Overview

Still Healing, but Beginning to Adjust

During Week 2, I developed a noticeable amount of swelling on the side of my quad, which may have been caused by the brace being too tight. My quad became extremely sore at times, and in some moments it actually hurt more than my knee itself.

Even though the deep shooting pain from Week 1 started to ease, the knee still felt heavy, stiff, and full of pressure. Most of the week was spent trying to control swelling and get through basic movement.

Swelling & Quad Pain

The Quad Became the Most Painful Part

The swelling on the side of my quad became much more noticeable. The soreness was intense, and at times it felt worse than the actual knee pain.

The deep shooting pain while standing started to decrease, but there was still a strong and constant pressure inside the knee.

Heavy support during Week 2: The brace and bandaging were still helping protect the knee while the swelling slowly settled.

A Noticeable Turning Point

Improvement Around Day 10

Around day 10, after starting a steroid medication, I noticed a very clear improvement. The swelling and pressure reduced significantly, and the knee finally felt slightly more manageable.

Mobility & Walking

Basic Movement Was Still Exhausting

I tried walking more using both crutches, but I was extremely fatigued. The Vicodin I was taking made me very tired, which made even basic movement feel exhausting.

Walking was still limited and required a lot of focus and effort, even with assistance from the brace and crutches.

Healing Progress

Seeing the Knee Without the Bandages

The bandages were off, and the incisions were beginning to heal. Even though the knee was still visibly swollen and stiff, seeing the surgical sites made the progress feel more real.

I continued icing four to five times a day and elevating my leg as much as possible to help control inflammation.

Early incision healing: The steri-strips were still in place while the swelling slowly began to improve.

Exercises & Physical Limitations

My Leg Still Felt Like Dead Weight

At this stage, the only exercise I could consistently do was ankle pumps. My leg still felt like dead weight, and I had no ability to lift it because my quad activation was still extremely weak or absent.

Mental Challenge

Feeling Stuck in the Same Place

Mentally, this period was very difficult. I was extremely bored, bed-ridden for nearly two weeks, and it often felt like recovery was stuck or not improving at all.

At times it felt discouraging, like progress might not come, even though small improvements were happening in the background.

A Small but Important Improvement

I finally took a shower sitting down, and the warm water helped relax my muscles. Afterward, my knee felt slightly looser and less stiff. It was a small moment, but it made the week feel a little more manageable.
