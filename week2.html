Week 2: The Adjustment Phase

During Week 2, I developed a noticeable amount of swelling on the side of my quad, which may have been caused by the brace being too tight. My quad became extremely sore at times, and in some moments it actually hurt more than my knee itself.

Swelling & Quad Pain

The swelling in my quad became more noticeable during this week, especially on the side. The soreness was intense, and at times it felt worse than the actual knee pain.

Despite this, the deep shooting pain I felt in Week 1 started to decrease when standing. However, there was still a strong and constant feeling of pressure inside the knee.

Around day 10, after starting a steroid medication, I noticed a very clear improvement. Swelling and pressure reduced significantly, and the knee felt slightly more manageable.

Mobility & Walking

I tried walking more using both crutches, but I was extremely fatigued. The Vicodin I was taking made me very tired, which made even basic movement exhausting.

Walking was still limited and required a lot of focus and effort, even with assistance.

Exercises & Physical Limitations

At this stage, the only exercise I could consistently do was ankle pumps. My leg still felt like dead weight, and I had no ability to lift it due to extremely weak or absent quad activation.

Swelling Management

Swelling in the knee was slowly going down over time. I was icing 4–5 times a day and elevating my leg as much as possible to control inflammation.

Mental Challenge

Mentally, this period was very difficult. I was extremely bored, bed-ridden for nearly two weeks, and it often felt like recovery was stuck or not improving at all.

At times it felt discouraging, like progress might not come, even though small improvements were happening.

Small Improvements

I finally took a shower sitting down, and the warm water helped relax my muscles. Afterward, my knee felt slightly looser and less stiff.

At this point, I still wasn’t confident enough to return to school because I didn’t want to risk pain, fatigue, or setbacks while being around a lot of movement.
