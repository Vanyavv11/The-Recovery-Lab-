During Week 2, I developed a noticeable amount of swelling on the side of my quad, which may have been caused by the brace being too tight. My quad became extremely sore at times, and in some moments it actually hurt more than my knee itself.

The deep shooting pain I felt in Week 1 started to decrease when standing, but there was still a strong feeling of pressure in the knee. Around day 10, after starting a steroid medication, I noticed a significant reduction in that pressure and overall discomfort.

I tried increasing how much I was walking using both crutches, but I was often very fatigued since the Vicodin I was taking made me extremely tired and low energy.

At this stage, the only exercise I was able to do consistently was ankle pumps. My leg still felt like dead weight, and I was unable to lift it at all due to lack of quad activation.

Swelling in the knee was slowly starting to go down. I was icing my knee 4–5 times a day and elevating it as much as possible to help with recovery.

Mentally, this period was very difficult. I was extremely bored and felt stuck being bed-ridden for nearly two weeks, and it often felt like the recovery process might not improve anytime soon.

I eventually took my first shower while sitting down, and the warm water seemed to relax my muscles. Afterward, my knee felt slightly looser and a bit less stiff.

At this point, I still wasn’t confident enough to return to school, as I didn’t want to risk increased pain or setbacks during recovery.
