Week 5-6: Major Milestones and Growing Confidence

Theme: First real return of quad function and early independence in movement.

Summary: First straight leg raise, walking without crutches around the house, reaching 90° of flexion, and returning to the gym and pool marked a major turning point in recovery.

Week 5 was one of the biggest weeks of my recovery so far. After weeks of struggling with quad activation and basic movement, I finally started seeing the results of all the rehab work. For the first time since surgery, recovery felt less focused on simply getting through the day and more focused on rebuilding strength and function.

Wins of the Week

  • First successful straight leg raise
  • Walking around the house without crutches
  • Reached 90° of knee flexion
  • Cleared to begin swimming
  • Returned to the gym
  • Strong improvement in quad activation

Post-Op Appointment

I had my five-week post-op appointment this week. My doctor was very happy with how the knee looked overall and noted that swelling was under control and recovery was progressing well.

At this point, my flexion had reached 90 degrees, and I was only a few degrees away from full extension. My doctor explained that the next major goal is achieving full extension and maintaining it consistently.

To help improve extension, he recommended prone hangs, which involve lying on your stomach and allowing your knees to hang slightly off the edge of a bed or table. His expectation was that by my next post-op appointment, I should have full extension and be approaching full flexion as well.

The First Straight Leg Raise

The biggest milestone of the week was finally achieving my first straight leg raise. After weeks of trying to wake up my quad with minimal activation, this was a major breakthrough.

For the first time, I could clearly see my quad activating during exercises like quad sets, which showed real progress in muscle recovery.

Walking Progress

I could walk around the house without crutches and felt stable enough that my knee no longer felt like it would give out during normal movement.

My physical therapist still recommended using at least one crutch for longer distances until my range of motion and strength improve further. Around the house I felt comfortable without assistance, but I continued using a crutch outside for safety.

Returning to the Gym

I returned to the gym for the first time since surgery, focusing mainly on rehab exercises and strengthening my hips and quads.

After my first session, my knee swelled and became more uncomfortable, which showed I had pushed a bit too hard. Since then, I have been more careful about gradually increasing intensity and listening to my knee.

Pool Therapy

I was cleared to swim this week, and once in the water my knee immediately felt lighter and easier to move.

I also tested light PT exercises in the pool, which felt surprisingly smooth due to reduced weight-bearing. It gave me confidence in my movement again.

Range of Motion

My flexion reached 90 degrees this week, marking a major improvement in range of motion. Extension was also improving, but I was still a few degrees away from fully straightening the knee.

Stairs were still challenging. I could go up them slowly, but going down remained difficult due to limited strength and mobility.

Still Working On

Full knee extension, consistent straight leg raises, and improving stair control remain the main focus moving forward.

Mental Progress

Mentally, this was one of the most encouraging weeks so far. Seeing real functional progress made the recovery feel less overwhelming and more achievable.

I felt more motivated and confident that I was finally moving in the right direction after weeks of slower progress.
