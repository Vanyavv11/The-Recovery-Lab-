How I Tore My ACL

I tore my ACL on December 24th, Christmas Eve, while on a ski trip in the Aspen mountains.

The Injury

It was the first run down the mountain. I went all in, skiing fast with a lot of confidence. The only things I remember are feeling extremely dizzy, wind and snow hitting my face, and my vision going blurry. Then everything went black for a moment as I fell.

Even though it only lasted a few seconds, I remember the impact very clearly. When I stopped rolling, I wasn’t immediately in sharp pain — I was more shocked, like I didn’t fully understand what had just happened.

When I finally processed it, I looked down and noticed one ski was completely gone. My left leg still had the ski attached, and I could feel a deep, full ache around my entire knee. It wasn’t a sharp pain, but a heavy, uncomfortable feeling that wrapped around the joint.

My left leg had twisted slightly with the ski still attached, which made everything feel worse. I tried to remove the ski, but I couldn’t do it on my own.

Getting Help

A ski coach who was coming down the mountain stopped to help me. He managed to remove the ski and helped me get back on my feet. However, I still felt very dizzy and almost immediately collapsed back into the snow.

I told him I was okay but just needed to rest. He stayed briefly and then had to continue teaching his ski group. Shortly after, my parents found me sitting in the snow.

At this point, I was still not in severe pain — mostly just confused and in shock. I genuinely thought it might just be a minor sprain.

Skiing After the Injury

After resting for a while, I continued skiing down the mountain. I didn’t fully realize how serious the injury was at that moment.

Once we got to the bottom, I took off my ski boot and tested my leg by putting weight on it. Something felt off — the knee felt unstable and loose, and it would occasionally give out under pressure. Still, there wasn’t a lot of pain, so I didn’t think too much of it.

I ended up skiing for another 1–2 hours that day.

The Next Day

The next morning was very different. I woke up with a swollen knee and noticeable pain. I could still get out of bed, but I was limping heavily.

We got ice from the hotel, and I spent most of the day resting in bed while taking Tylenol to manage the discomfort.

At this point, I started researching injuries on my own and initially thought it might be a torn meniscus.

Reflection

Looking back, this was the moment everything started. At the time, I didn’t fully understand the severity of the injury, but the instability and swelling the next day were early signs that something more serious had happened.
