Week 4: Major Progress and Motivation Boost

Summary: Major improvements in flexion, reduced to one crutch, and first real signs of functional independence.

Week 4 brought a huge amount of improvement, and I felt genuinely happy with how my recovery was progressing. For the first time, it started to feel like I was making real, visible progress week to week.

Wins of the Week

  • Flexion improved from ~40° to ~70°
  • Down to one crutch
  • First time lifting leg to the side
  • More independence in daily activities
  • Return to more normal movement around daily life

Pain & Daily Comfort

Pain was basically gone during the day. The only discomfort I experienced was in the mornings when I first woke up, which was mostly due to stiffness from not moving overnight. This was expected and felt like a normal part of recovery rather than actual injury pain.

Flexion & Range of Motion

One of the biggest milestones this week was a major improvement in knee flexion. I went from around 40 degrees to approximately 70 degrees in just one week, which was a huge jump and very motivating.

Even though bending still felt tight at times, seeing that level of improvement made me want to keep pushing through rehab consistently.

Strength & Leg Control

I was finally able to lift my leg out to the side, which was a big functional milestone. While I still could not perform a straight leg raise, being able to lift my leg sideways made everyday movements much easier, such as getting in and out of cars and bed on my own.

However, my leg still felt weak when trying to walk without support, and it would occasionally give out, so I continued using crutches for stability.

Mobility & Crutches

I progressed down to using only one crutch, which was a major step forward. I was able to put significantly more weight through my injured leg, and walking felt more natural compared to previous weeks.

Although I was still not fully independent, this was a clear sign of improvement in balance and strength.

Physical Therapy & Exercises

I continued with my basic rehabilitation exercises and also started using the NMES (neuromuscular electrical stimulation) machine during physical therapy sessions to help activate my quad muscle.

The goal was to “wake up” the quad so I could eventually regain full control and begin lifting my leg straight again.

Extension Progress

Knee extension continued to improve, although it was sometimes harder to maintain after focusing heavily on flexion exercises. Despite this, I noticed my knee becoming straighter while walking, which was a clear improvement.

Life & Activity

I started returning to more normal activities again. I went to the beach and was walking around more throughout the day, which made recovery feel less restrictive.

Still Working On

Straight leg raise, full quad activation, and walking without crutches are still in progress. These remain the main goals moving into the next phase of recovery.

Mental Progress

Mentally, this week felt like a major shift. The progress I made made the journey feel less impossible and more achievable. I felt more motivated than before and excited to keep pushing forward in rehab.

I was hopeful that I would soon be walking without crutches and continuing to build strength week by week.
