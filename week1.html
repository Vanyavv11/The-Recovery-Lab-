Week 1

What started as a focus on reducing swelling and regaining mobility quickly became a much more difficult process than expected, with significant pain and stiffness during early rehabilitation.

In the first few days, I was not able to do any real exercises at all. There was essentially zero quad activation, and even basic movement felt extremely difficult.

Day-to-day life was very limited — I was barely able to walk, and most movement came with a lot of discomfort. Standing up often caused sharp, shooting pain in my knee along with a strong feeling of pressure. The doctor explained this was likely due to fluid shifting around inside the joint after surgery.

I also had a nerve block on day 1, which meant I didn’t feel much immediately after surgery. However, once it wore off in the middle of the night, the pain set in heavily and that was when the true recovery process began.

Early rehab was focused mainly on managing swelling, trying to regain basic movement, and slowly beginning to re-engage the quad as pain allowed.
