Week 1: The Beginning of Recovery

What started as a focus on reducing swelling and regaining mobility quickly became a much more difficult process than expected, with significant pain and stiffness during early rehabilitation.

Early Post-Op Experience

In the first few days after surgery, I was not able to do any real exercises at all. There was essentially zero quad activation, and my entire leg felt like dead weight due to how much muscle had shut down.

A large part of my time was spent dealing with pain, pressure, and stiffness in the knee. I was barely able to walk, and even standing up often caused sharp, shooting pain along with a strong pressure sensation. My doctor explained this was likely due to fluid shifting around inside the joint after surgery.

Nerve Block & Pain Onset

I had a nerve block on day 1, which meant I didn’t feel much immediately after surgery. However, once it wore off in the middle of the night, the pain became very intense and that was when the real recovery process began.

One-Week Post-Op Appointment

At my one-week post-op appointment, my doctor removed the bandages and steri-strips. Seeing my knee for the first time was overwhelming — there were two larger incisions and three smaller ones.

I had chosen a quadriceps tendon graft, and it was clear how much had been done inside the knee. At this stage, all of my quad muscle had essentially shut down, making my leg feel completely like dead weight.

Medication & Pain Management

I was taking a prescribed medication called Vicodin, but it didn’t significantly reduce pain even when taking two doses. However, it did make me very sleepy around 4–5 hours after taking it, which is why I often relied on it at night.

Because the pain and pressure were still strong, my doctor prescribed a stronger 5-day steroid, which I took daily. This noticeably helped reduce swelling and pressure over the following days.

Early Recovery Plan

They also recommended that I start physical therapy as soon as possible to begin restoring movement and muscle activation.

Early rehab was focused on managing swelling, trying to regain basic movement, and slowly beginning to re-engage the quad as pain allowed.
