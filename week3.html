Week 3: The First Signs of Progress

Week 3 marked a major turning point in my recovery. The pain had decreased significantly, and most of the deep pressure inside my knee was finally gone. The soreness in my quad also improved considerably, making daily activities much more manageable than they had been during the first two weeks.

Pain & Swelling

One of the biggest improvements during Week 3 was the reduction in pain and swelling. The deep pressure sensation that had been present since surgery was almost completely gone, and the swelling throughout my knee had noticeably decreased.

Although the knee still felt stiff, it was encouraging to finally see visible improvements and feel less discomfort throughout the day.

First Physical Therapy Evaluation

This week I attended my first physical therapy appointment, where they assessed my current progress and established a plan for rehabilitation.

During my evaluation, my knee flexion measured 40 degrees. Stiffness, swelling, and muscle guarding were the main factors limiting my range of motion at this stage. While it was clear there was still a long way to go, having a baseline measurement gave me a starting point and clear goals moving forward.

My therapist emphasized the importance of regaining full knee extension early in the recovery process, explaining that it is one of the most critical milestones after ACL surgery.

Quad Activation Challenges

Despite improvements in pain, my quad remained almost completely inactive. I still could not lift my leg straight up or perform side leg raises on my own.

When attempting quad sets, there was little to no visible muscle activation. My leg continued to feel weak and disconnected, making even simple movements difficult.

Rehabilitation Exercises

I began performing the exercises provided by my physical therapist on a daily basis. These included heel slides to improve knee flexion, weight-shifting exercises to gradually increase confidence on my injured leg, frequent quad sets to encourage muscle activation, and extension exercises focused on restoring full knee straightening.

While the exercises were simple, consistency became the main focus during this stage of recovery.

Returning to School

One of the biggest milestones this week was returning to school. I was finally able to stop taking all pain medication and begin getting back into a more normal routine.

Although I still needed both crutches at all times, being back at school felt like a significant step forward. Walking remained exhausting, but it was a reminder that recovery was progressing, even if it felt slow.

Mental Battle

With less pain, I felt more hopeful about recovery. However, I still found myself frustrated when comparing my progress to others online who seemed to regain flexion, extension, and straight-leg raises faster than I did.

Over time, I realized that every recovery timeline is different. Comparing myself to others only made the process more frustrating, so I focused on controlling what I could, staying consistent with rehab, and trusting that progress would come with time.
