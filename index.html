ACL Recovery Journey

From playing sports on a torn ACL to rebuilding strength through surgery and rehab, this is a real-time comeback journey.

About This Journey

This blog documents my recovery process after an ACL injury, including rehab progress, physical changes, mental challenges, and overall return-to-sport journey.

Before Surgery

Even while dealing with a torn ACL, I was still able to continue playing sports through intense rehab, strengthening, and a very focused mindset. Over time, my knee reached a point where it felt relatively stable and somewhat normal during daily movement and even light athletic activity.

Physically, it became manageable — but mentally, it was a completely different challenge. There was always a constant awareness that one wrong movement could lead to reinjury or long-term damage.

That uncertainty became the deciding factor. Even though I felt capable of competing, especially during CIF badminton championships, the fear of something going wrong at the worst possible moment made it difficult to fully commit and trust my knee.

In the end, the biggest limitation wasn’t just the injury itself — it was the mental battle of confidence, control, and risk.

Week 1

What started as a focus on reducing swelling and regaining mobility quickly became a more difficult process than expected, with significant pain and stiffness during early rehabilitation.

Early rehab focused on controlling swelling, restoring basic movement, and trying to re-activate my quad after surgery.

What I’ve Learned So Far

Recovery is not linear — some days feel like progress, others feel like setbacks.

The mental side of recovery is just as hard as the physical side, especially when trying to trust your knee again.

Small improvements matter more than they feel like in the moment.

Consistency in rehab is what drives long-term recovery.
